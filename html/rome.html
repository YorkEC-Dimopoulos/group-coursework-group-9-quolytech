753 BC: Foundation of Rome

According to legend, twin brothers Romulus and Remus, raised by a she-wolf, founded Rome. Romulus killed Remus and became the first king, beginning Rome's history as a monarchy.

Roman Civilization

Overview

Rome grew from a small city-state to a vast empire spanning three continents. Its legacy includes law, language, architecture, and engineering.

  • Three periods: Kingdom, Republic, Empire
  • Capital: Rome (later Constantinople)
  • Latin language ancestor of Romance languages
  • Famous for roads, aqueducts, and concrete

Society & Government

Roman society was hierarchical but allowed social mobility. Government evolved from monarchy to republic to imperial autocracy.

  • Patricians (aristocrats) vs Plebeians (commoners)
  • Republican government with Senate
  • Professional army (legions)
  • Roman citizenship with rights and duties

Culture & Innovations

Romans were great engineers and adapters of Greek culture. They created lasting innovations in architecture, law, and urban planning.

  • Architecture: arches, domes, concrete
  • Engineering: aqueducts, roads, sanitation
  • Roman law basis for many legal systems
  • Gladiatorial games and chariot races

Important Figures

From statesmen to emperors, these individuals shaped Roman history and left lasting legacies.

  • Julius Caesar: General who ended Republic
  • Augustus: First emperor, brought peace
  • Cicero: Statesman, orator, philosopher
  • Constantine: First Christian emperor

Roman Achievements

Engineering

Romans built over 400,000 km of roads, aqueducts that supplied cities with water, and advanced sanitation systems.

Law

Roman law formed the basis for civil law systems in most of Europe and influenced common law as well.

Architecture

The Pantheon, Colosseum, and Roman baths demonstrate architectural innovation that still inspires today.
