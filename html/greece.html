Greek Civilization

Overview

Ancient Greece was a collection of city-states located in the northeastern Mediterranean. Greek culture influenced the Roman Empire and later Western civilization.

  • City-states (Athens, Sparta, Corinth)
  • Colonization throughout Mediterranean
  • Olympic Games starting in 776 BC
  • Development of alphabet from Phoenician

Culture

Greek culture emphasized philosophy, arts, and athletics. They made significant contributions to literature, drama, architecture, and science.

  • Theater (tragedy and comedy)
  • Classical architecture (columns, temples)
  • Philosophy (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle)
  • Historical writing (Herodotus, Thucydides)

Religion

Greek religion was polytheistic with gods who had human-like qualities. Mount Olympus was considered the home of the gods.

  • Olympian gods (Zeus, Athena, Apollo)
  • Oracle at Delphi
  • Mythology explaining natural phenomena
  • Religious festivals and games

Important People

Ancient Greece produced many influential figures in philosophy, politics, and the arts.

  • Pericles: Athenian statesman during Golden Age
  • Socrates: Philosopher who questioned everything
  • Alexander the Great: Conquered vast empire
  • Homer: Epic poet (Iliad and Odyssey)
