Upper and Lower Egypt unite under Pharaoh Narmer (Menes), beginning the Early Dynastic Period. This marks the start of ancient Egyptian civilization as we know it.

Egyptian Civilization

Overview

Ancient Egypt was a civilization of ancient Northeast Africa concentrated along the lower reaches of the Nile River. It lasted for over 3000 years, making it one of the longest-lasting civilizations in history.

  • Located along the Nile River
  • Three main kingdoms: Old, Middle, New
  • Advanced agriculture based on Nile floods
  • Hieroglyphic writing system

Culture

Egyptian culture was deeply religious with a complex polytheistic belief system. Art and architecture followed strict conventions that changed little over millennia.

  • Distinctive art with symbolic colors
  • Monumental architecture (pyramids, temples)
  • Advancements in mathematics and medicine
  • Papyrus for writing and record keeping

Religion

Religion was central to Egyptian life. They believed in an afterlife and developed complex burial practices to ensure safe passage to the next world.

  • Polytheistic with hundreds of deities
  • Pharaoh considered a living god
  • Mummification to preserve bodies
  • Book of the Dead as a burial guide

Important People

From pharaohs to architects, many individuals left their mark on Egyptian history.

  • Khufu: Pharaoh who built the Great Pyramid
  • Hatshepsut: Female pharaoh, great builder
  • Tutankhamun: Boy king with intact tomb
  • Imhotep: Architect of first pyramid
